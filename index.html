STL Dissect: 3D Paper Printing

What if you could make high speed, low-resolution 3D printed parts with no more hardware than a common paper printer? What if you could make a 3D print in seconds rather than hours?

Preview:

How to use Dissect STL

With nothing more than a common copy printer and STL Dissect, you can produce high speed, low resolution printed objects. The process is simple.
Upload your STL file as you would to a traditional 3D printer. Adjust your part to your preferred specifications (these features are still being developed). Then export your file as a PDF and print on any inkjet or laser printer. Once you have printed your part on a 8.5 x 11 sheet of paper, you can easily cut and fold the piece into your desired part.
These kinds of low resolution, high speed models are ideal for creative ideation and quick iteration. 3D Paper Printing enhances the design process by allowing designers to quickly visualize, critique, and improve "looks like" prototypes. It also makes 3D printing accessible when traditional 3D printers are not available.
